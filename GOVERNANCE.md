## üë• Roles and Responsibilities

We recognize that contributions come in many forms‚Äînot just code. We value documentation, design, research, and community management equally.

### 1. Contributors
Everyone who interacts with the project is a Contributor.
* **Who they are:** Anyone who comments on an issue, submits a Pull Request, edits the Wiki, or helps others in discussions.
* **Rights:** To have your voice heard, your contributions reviewed fairly, and to be treated with respect.

### 2. Maintainers
Maintainers are Contributors who have shown dedication and responsibility.
* **Who they are:** Active contributors who have been invited to join the organization with "Write" access.
* **Responsibilities:**
    * Triage issues and review Pull Requests.
    * Merge approved changes.
    * Ensure code quality and security standards.
    * Foster a welcoming environment.

### 3. Project Leads
Project Leads are responsible for the overall direction and health of the project.
* **Responsibilities:**
    * Setting the high-level roadmap and vision.
    * Managing project infrastructure (servers, keys, domains).
    * resolving final disputes when consensus cannot be reached.
    * Ensuring the Code of Conduct is enforced.

## ‚öñÔ∏è Decision Making Process

We operate on a model of **"Lazy Consensus."**

### How it works
You don't need to ask permission for every little thing. If you want to improve something, just do it!
1.  **Propose a change** (via an Issue or Pull Request).
2.  **Wait for feedback.**
3.  **Silence is approval.** If no one objects within a reasonable timeframe (usually 72 hours for bigger changes), it is assumed that the community supports the change.

### Major Changes (RFC Process)
For significant changes‚Äîlike altering the database architecture, adding a new blockchain integration, or changing the UI paradigm‚Äîwe use a **Request for Comments (RFC)** process:
1.  Open an issue labeled `proposal`.
2.  Outline the problem and your proposed solution.
3.  Invite discussion.
4.  Maintainers will summarize the consensus before moving forward.

### Dispute Resolution
We aim for consensus. If there is a disagreement:
1.  We discuss the technical merits and community impact first.
2.  If a compromise cannot be reached, the **Project Leads** will make the final decision based on the project's Mission and Values.
